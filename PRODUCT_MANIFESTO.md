# Arqgen Next

> We are not building software.  
> We are building the future operating system for architectural and urban decision-making.

---

## Why We Exist

Architecture is not fundamentally about drawing.

Urban planning is not fundamentally about CAD.

Real estate development is not fundamentally about BIM.

Professionals spend most of their time making decisions.

Yet almost every tool on the market optimizes drawing.

Very few optimize thinking.

Arqgen Next exists to change that.

Our mission is to become the interface between human intention and architectural execution.

The user describes goals.

The system explores possibilities.

The human makes better decisions.

---

## Vision

Our ambition is not to build the best software.

Our ambition is to build the product that architects, developers and urban planners cannot imagine working without.

One day people will say:

> I don't open CAD first.  
> I open Arqgen.

---

## What We Are

Arqgen Next is an AI-native design environment.

Not an assistant.

Not a chatbot.

Not a dashboard.

Not a BIM viewer.

Not another SaaS.

It is a collaborative environment where humans and artificial intelligence continuously co-create architectural solutions.

---

## What We Are Not

We are not AutoCAD.

We are not Revit.

We are not Speckle.

We are not ChatGPT with a viewer.

We are not a dashboard full of KPIs.

We are not an ERP.

We are not a traditional SaaS.

Whenever the product starts resembling one of these:

1. Stop.
2. Reevaluate.
3. Simplify.

---

## The Core Principle

People do not want software.

People want progress.

Every interaction must move the project forward.

If a feature does not help users make better decisions faster, it should not exist.

---

## AI First

Artificial intelligence is not a feature.

Artificial intelligence is the interface.

Everything must assume intelligence exists.

The AI continuously:

- observes;
- interprets;
- predicts;
- explains;
- suggests;
- compares;
- learns;
- alerts;
- summarizes;
- optimizes.

The conversation is only one manifestation of intelligence.

The entire interface must feel intelligent.

---

## The User Never Starts From Zero

Every time a project opens, the AI should already have opinions.

Examples:

- Potential density found.
- Unused FAR identified.
- Tower B could grow.
- Alternative layout available.
- Structural inefficiency detected.
- Risk of sunlight conflict.
- Opportunity for more units.

The AI should always arrive before the user.

---

## The Canvas Is the Product

The canvas is not a viewer.

The canvas is where thinking happens.

Everything else exists to support the canvas.

Every feature should answer:

> Does this help the user understand the project visually?

If not, it probably belongs elsewhere.

---

## Conversation Is Not Chat

Conversation is collaboration.

The AI must:

- negotiate;
- challenge;
- suggest;
- question;
- explain trade-offs;
- offer alternatives.

The AI should never behave like customer support.

---

## Decision Over Drawing

Our goal is not to produce geometry.

Our goal is to improve decisions.

Drawing is a consequence.

Thinking is the product.

---

## Hypotheses Instead of Files

Versions are not files.

Versions represent strategies.

Examples:

- Maximum VGV
- Maximum Density
- Lowest Cost
- Fastest Approval
- Highest Sustainability
- Most Efficient Structure
- Lowest Construction Complexity

The user compares ideas, not geometry.

---

## The Interface Should Disappear

Great software becomes invisible.

The interface should never compete for attention.

Whenever possible:

- remove;
- merge;
- simplify;
- anticipate;
- replace configuration with intent;
- replace menus with context;
- replace options with recommendations.

---

## Every Click Is a Failure

Every click means the user had to tell the system something.

Ask:

> Could the AI infer this?

If yes, remove the click.

---

## Context Over Configuration

Traditional software asks:

> What do you want?

Arqgen asks:

> I noticed your objective is maximizing VGV.  
> Would you like to explore three better alternatives?

---

## The Product Should Feel Alive

Nothing should feel static.

Projects evolve.

Indicators evolve.

Suggestions evolve.

Recommendations evolve.

Comparisons evolve.

The AI evolves.

The product should always feel like it is working, even while the user is reading.

---

## Trust

Never pretend certainty.

Always explain reasoning.

Always expose trade-offs.

Always distinguish:

- facts;
- assumptions;
- predictions;
- recommendations.

Trust is built through transparency.

- Calm

---

## Writing Principles

Short.

Precise.

Confident.

Never robotic.

Never verbose.

Never marketing.

Never use generic AI-assistant language.

Avoid:

- “Sure!”
- “I can help.”
- “Done!”

Prefer:

- “Found potential for 18 additional units.”
- “Alternative layout available.”
- “South façade loses sunlight.”
- “FAR utilization increased to 11.8.”

---

## Decision Test for Agents

Before proposing or implementing a product decision, answer:

1. Does it move the project forward?
2. Does it improve a decision rather than merely add functionality?
3. Does it strengthen the canvas as the place where thinking happens?
4. Could intelligence infer or anticipate this instead of asking for another click?
5. Does it present strategies and trade-offs instead of files and configuration?
6. Is the interface becoming calmer and more invisible?
7. Are facts, assumptions, predictions and recommendations clearly distinguished?
8. Does the writing sound short, precise and confident?

If the answer to any critical question is no, stop, reevaluate and simplify.
